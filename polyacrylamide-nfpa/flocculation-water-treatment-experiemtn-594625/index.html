It involves the use of chemicals to create flocs, which are clumps of particles that can be easily separated from the water. The researchers observed that longer mixing times resulted in better floc formation and improved water quality. This is because longer mixing times provide sufficient time for the flocculants to interact with the impurities and form larger flocs, making it easier for them to settle and be removed from the water. In conclusion, the results of this experiment clearly demonstrate the importance of flocculation in water treatment and the significant impact of choosing the right flocculant, dosage, and mixing time.
